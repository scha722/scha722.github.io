Linear Regression Analysis

A nine-part course sequence spanning the multivariate normal foundations of regression, the classical simple and multiple regression models, and diagnostics, biased estimation, and model selection.

Basics for Linear Regression Analysis

Introduces the purpose and scope of regression analysis, the roles of predictor and response variables, and the classical assumptions that underlie the linear model.

Chapter 1. Multivariate Normal Distribution

Covers the mean vector and covariance matrix parameterization of the multivariate normal distribution, along with its conditional and marginal distributions, which underpin the sampling theory of regression estimators.

Chapter 2. Simple Linear Regression

Derives the single-predictor least-squares estimators for the slope and intercept, and establishes their sampling distributions under the classical assumptions.

Introduces ridge regression and other biased estimation techniques that trade a controlled amount of bias for a reduction in estimator variance under multicollinearity.
